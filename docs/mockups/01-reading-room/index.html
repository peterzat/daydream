The Stopped Clock

the dream · chapter one

a watercolour of the hour that stopped

You come to rest at the base of a great stopped clock, its brass face gone still and patient, as if it had decided the hour could keep a while. Dust turns slow in a shaft of late gold. A tall glass-fronted case† stands against one wall, its little door minding its own quiet secrets; and on a lectern a worn ledger waits open — as though expecting someone, you perhaps, to come and turn a page.

the ledger, opened

“The great clock has stood still since the first hard frost. The fault is the escapement gear — the small brass heart of the whole mechanism — which slipped its pin one cold night and rolled away down the lane, south, toward the old well.”

†A small brass lock holds the little door fast.
