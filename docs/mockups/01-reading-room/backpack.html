Keepsakes

things carried, things found — a traveller’s pressed collection

in your satchel
the escapement gear
a small brass gear, its fine teeth flecked with green, cool from the shadow but sound and whole.
an errand
found — down the lane, toward the old well
a pressed firefly-lantern
its glass long cold and its small light remembered — kept for the comfort of it.
a keepsake
found — a summer you almost recall
your satchel, worn crosswise
the collection
empty
room for what you’ll find
empty
a place for a small thing
empty
not yet gathered
empty
for a curio, perhaps

keepsakes gather quietly as you wander —
every dream leaves you carrying a little more.
