## Verdict

**This finetune does not fit daydream's data-skill pipeline shape.** All five narrate sections below show the `daydream.skills.data` "applied empty" fallback (`The dream is quiet; nothing stirs just yet.`) because the model returns JSON-valid but content-empty output. A direct probe at vLLM (logged in the SPEC 2026-05-06 commit body) shows the raw response is `{"effects":[{}]}` — a single effect with no `kind` and no `text`, which fails the effect-allowlist validation, leaves `applied` empty, and triggers the soft fallback narrate. Without the strict-JSON `response_format` constraint, the same model produces verbose free-form roleplay continuation that overshoots the "1-2 sentences" instruction (200 tokens of narrative monologue in a smoke test). The MN-12b-RP-Ink finetune is trained for prose continuation; its structured-output capability is degraded relative to the Mistral Nemo base.

**Voice-quality comparison against the 2026-05-06 AWQ baseline is therefore moot for this candidate.** A meaningful next-turn A/B would need either (a) a creative-writing finetune that preserves JSON-following capability (uncommon among the published RP-Ink-class models, which optimize for unstructured prose), (b) a daydream pipeline change that accepts free-form narrate prose and post-parses, or (c) a fairer base-vs-finetune A/B (Mistral Nemo Instruct vs. Mistral Nemo + RP-Ink) that factors out the base-architecture axis so the finetune contribution is isolated.

**Latency is not directly comparable across the two captures.** Wall-times below are measured with vLLM's automatic prefix caching warm; the AWQ baseline at `2026-05-06-qwen2.5-7b-instruct-awq.md` was measured cold. The intrinsic 12B-Q4-on-Mistral-Nemo cold-start latency on this hardware was ~6-8 s per prompt during the first capture (vs ~1.5 s for 7B-AWQ cold), tracking the localreview "doubling weight bytes is strictly worse than halving KV cache" lesson on memory-bandwidth-bound autoregressive decode. Output-token counts (7 each) reflect the empty-effect JSON, not generation throughput.

**vLLM flags note.** `max_model_len` is `4096` here vs `8192` in the AWQ baseline — the documented "GGUF-specific deviation" the spec anticipated. At `--gpu-memory-utilization 0.45` (~9 GiB on the 20 GiB card), Q4_K_M weights leave only ~1.11 GiB for KV cache; vLLM's startup probe reports 8192 needs 1.25 GiB and refuses, with an estimated ceiling of 7248. 4096 is well within budget and far above this corpus's actual ~1100-token-per-call usage.
